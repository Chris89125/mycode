TO PLAY

The READER asks each WRITER in turn to call out a word—an adjective or a noun or whatever the space calls for—and uses them to fill in the blank spaces in the story. The result is a MAD LIBS® game. When the READER then reads the completed MAD LIBS® game to the other players, they will discover that they have written a story that is fantastic, screamingly funny, shocking, silly, crazy, or just plain dumb—depending upon which words each WRITER called out.

Quick Review

1. An ADJECTIVE describes something or somebody.
Lumpy, soft, ugly, messy, and short are all adjectives.

2. An ADVERB tells how something is done.
It usually modifies a verb (but sometimes an adjective or
another adverb) and usually ends in “ly.”
Modestly, stupidly, greedily, carefully, and very are all adverbs.

3. A NOUN is the name of a person, place, thing or concept.
Sidewalk, umbrella, bridle, bathtub, nose, and love are all nouns.

4. A VERB is an action word (or state). Run, pitch, jump, swim, and am are all verbs.
Put the verbs in past tense if the directions say PAST TENSE.
Ran, pitched, jumped, swam, and was are the same verbs in the past tense.

5. A PLACE can be any sort of place: a country, or city (Spain, Delta)
or a room (bathroom, kitchen), or a location (zoo, racetrack)

6. An EXCLAMATION or SILLY WORD is any sort of
funny sound, gasp, grunt, or outcry, like Wow!, Ouch!, Whomp!,
Ick!, and Gadzooks!
